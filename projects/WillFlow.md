# WillFlow

WillFlow mainly asks:

```text
When a community gradually owns public resources, means of production, and verifiable supply capability, how can every member periodically regain real influence over resource flow, and how can the use, expansion, and movement of resources remain continuously calibrated by the shared will of all members?
```

WillFlow currently begins as a public-will sandbox.

The sandbox can model:

```text
members;
organizations;
public resource pools;
means of production;
periodic public purchasing power;
products and services;
AI Agent-assisted choices;
old-weight correction;
main-chain baseline rules;
sub-chain autonomy;
dynamic use of production resources;
and rules against distortion by information gates, strong organizations, old accumulation, or AI Agents.
```

The purpose is to observe how resources flow, how organizations rise and fall, whether member will is truly expressed, and where the system drifts away from shared member will.

WillFlow's real validation ultimately depends on verifiable supply loops such as ProvisionLoop.

Before that, simulation can still make the problem visible, let mechanisms run, expose risks, and help participants enter concrete scenarios instead of facing only an abstract idea.
