How to Read This Page

Key Terms

Short explanations for human review. The machine-readable protocol still uses stable JSON keys, status codes, and policy route codes.

Root authority
The organization's authority record for OrgAnchor verification. It lists the root member keys and signature threshold used to verify current statements.
Statement
The signed official endpoint declaration. It says which website, verify page, security contact, and related public entry points currently belong to the organization.
Signature threshold
How many authorized root-member signatures are required before a statement is accepted as valid.
Agent Verification View
A human-readable version of the same first-pass state that AI agents read from organchor.json.
Overall status
A summary of identity, evidence, and conformance checks. It is not an endorsement of the organization.
Identity status
Whether the signed statement, hashes, signatures, and root authority checks passed.
Value status
Whether the signed claims and evidence package is present and whether obvious evidence gaps were found.
Conformance
How completely this package matches the OrgAnchor verification contract expected by tools and AI agents.
Trust decision
The final reliance decision. OrgAnchor does not assign it; the requesting person, organization, directory, or AI agent applies its own policy.
External Policy Route
A machine-readable hint telling external agents whether to stop, review warnings, request more evidence, or continue with their own policy.
S1 First-party
Evidence submitted by the organization itself. Useful for scope and integrity, but not independent by itself.
S2 Third-party
Third-party-looking material such as certification or lab reports, preferably with a recheck route and clear scope.
S3 Sampling
Evidence from random purchase, random sampling, or real market/customer-site sample checks, designed to reduce hand-picked sample risk.
S4 Observation
Real-world use, delivery, support, uptime, repair, or supply-continuity observation over time. In Fireseed Alpha this is design preview.
S5 Challenge
Public challenge, negative evidence, correction, dispute lifecycle, and historical accountability. In Fireseed Alpha this is design preview.
Carrier receipts
Records showing where artifacts were mirrored, archived, pinned, or timestamped. Carriers help durability, but they are not the identity root.
Lockfile
A publication ledger for carrier receipts and artifact hashes. When signed by the root authority, it becomes a tamper-evident snapshot of publication history.
